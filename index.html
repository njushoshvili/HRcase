Topic 02

Role of HR

Current Reality

1:70 Ratio: One HR manager for 70 employees creates a bottleneck.

CEO-Driven: Strategic decisions are made by the CEO. HR executes.

Service Provider Model: HR focuses on admin and ops, lacking a strategic seat.

Policy vs Practice

Internal regulations exist on paper but are rarely used.

Evaluation & Recommendation

While HR supports the CEO's decisions, it lacks a Strategic Role. The company should move from a "Service Provider" model to a "Strategic Partner" model.

The Path Forward:

By automating administrative tasks, HR can focus on Workforce Planning.

Job Analysis & Descriptions

The Reality: "Paper vs. Practice"

Formal Documents Only: Official job descriptions exist but are rarely used.

"Managerial Heartbeat": Responsibilities defined by daily urgency, not roles.

No Structure: No defined levels (Junior/Senior). Growth is subjective.

Risks of Ambiguity

  • Role Confusion: Who owns what?
  • Subjective Evaluation: Reviews based on opinion.
  • Burnout: Overloading compliant employees.

Prevents "Managerial Heartbeat" and ensures fair evaluations.

Topic 07

Performance Management

Current Challenges

  • No Clear Structure: Evaluations are informal conversations based on personal opinions rather than data.
  • Unrealistic KPIs: Goals are often vague or changed mid-process, leading to confusion.
  • Rater Bias: Managers suffer from the "Halo Effect" (one good trait masks poor performance) or "Recency Effect" (judging only recent work).

Recommendation: BARS

Move to Behaviorally Anchored Rating Scales (BARS) or MBO. This reduces bias by linking ratings to specific, observable behaviors.

Why it is better:

Structured evaluations make the process developmental, provide a legal basis for decisions, and ensure fairness across teams.

Topic 08

Compensation & Benefits

Current Reality: Hygiene Factors

OptimoGroup offers standard benefits (Salary, Health Insurance, VITA card). However, according to Herzberg‚Äôs Two-Factor Theory, these are merely hygiene factors. Their presence prevents dissatisfaction but does not actively motivate.

The Equity Problem

Raises happen only via individual negotiation. This creates perceived unfairness and stress for employees who dislike negotiating.

Recommendation: Total Rewards

Adopt a Total Rewards Model that includes intangible motivators like autonomy, mastery, and purpose.

Why it is better:

A transparent Pay-for-Performance system ensures Internal Equity, reducing the need for stressful individual negotiations.
